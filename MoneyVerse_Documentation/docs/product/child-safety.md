# Child Safety Requirements

## Non-negotiable protections

- No public child profiles
- No direct messaging with strangers
- No public comments
- No precise location
- No public school name
- No full legal name required
- No targeted advertising
- No gambling or randomized paid rewards
- No pressure to invite others
- No humiliation or wealth comparison
- No child-initiated bank or payment connection

## Identity and sharing

Children use non-identifying nicknames and avatars. Share cards omit names, school, location, actual financial amounts, user IDs, and embedded trackers. Younger users require guardian approval before external sharing.

## Emotional safety

Mistakes are reversible learning moments. The application may explain consequences but must not shame, frighten, or punish children for family circumstances.

## Parent and teacher boundaries

Parents can manage their family only. Teachers can see assigned educational progress but not family financial data. School administrators receive aggregate information unless a specific lawful and authorized need exists.

## User-generated content

Initial release should minimize free text. Do not implement chat, public posting, arbitrary uploads, or unrestricted profile biographies.

## Escalation

Potential child-safety incidents require:

1. Immediate restriction of the relevant feature or content.
2. Evidence preservation with minimal data exposure.
3. Human review.
4. Guardian or institutional escalation where appropriate.
5. Documented resolution.
